How I think about products

From observation to launch.

Whisperloop didn't start with a feature list. It started with a real problem I noticed.

→
Start with what's actually broken
Students have things they want to say but can't — not even anonymously in text, because screenshots exist. Voice felt different. That observation came first, the app second.
→
Prototype before building
Built a fully working interactive prototype — real mic recording, live waveforms, voice masking — before touching backend infrastructure. You need to feel a product before you commit to building it.
→
Safety isn't a version 2 problem
Anonymous apps get abused. I built the report system, auto-moderation logic, and privacy policy into the first version. That's not extra work — that's just what responsible product building looks like.
→
Validate with real people, not assumptions
120+ students, 52 form responses, and a bunch of in-person conversations. The feedback changed what I'm building next. That's the point of research — not to confirm what you already think.
